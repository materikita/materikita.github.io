1. Dinesh and Kelvin collected some sunflower seeds. The ratio of Dinesh'sseeds to Kelvin's seeds was 9:5. Dinesh gave 45 seeds to Kelvin. The ratioof Dinesh's seeds to Kelvin's seeds became 3:5.How many sunflower seeds did Dinesh have at first?b How many sunflower seeds did Kevin have in the end?​


Jawab:

a. Dinesh had 108 seeds at first.

b. Kelvin had 105 in the end.

Penjelasan dengan langkah-langkah:

Let's use D to represent the number of Dinesh's seed originally. And K to represent Kelvin's.

D:K = 9:5

D = 1.8K

Then Dinesh gave 45 seed to Kelvin and the ratio became 3:5.

D - 45 : K + 45 = 3:5

(D-45) = 0.6 (K + 45)

D - 45 = 0.6K + 27

D = 0.6K + 72

1.8 K = 0.6K + 72

1.2K = 72

K = 60

D = 1.8K = 1.8 x 60 = 108

a. Dinesh had 108 seeds at first.

b. Kelvin had 105 in the end.

20. How to plant flower seeds bhasa ingggris


Preparing Containers


   Image titled Grow Flowers from Seed Step 1

   1

   Choose the appropriate container. There are various options at affordable prices. The germinating flat is quite cheap and specifically made for germinating seeds. However, recycled plastic cups and egg cartons can also be obtained at affordable prices. Small flower pots can also be used.

   Image titled Grow Flowers from Seed Step 2

   2

   Make sure your container has enough room for seedling development. The best container has a depth of more than 10 cm because it allows the development of the plant root system.

   Image titled Grow Flowers from Seed Step 3

   3

   Sterilize containers. If you use an old plastic pot or tray, wet it with a 1/10 bleach solution and 9/10 water, then rinse thoroughly. This is not needed if you use a new container.

   Image titled Grow Flowers from Seed Step 4

   4

   Provide adequate watering. Most of the pots and sprout trays already have holes in the bottom for water to pass through. For users of plastic cups, egg cartons, or other non-perforated containers, puncture one or two small holes in the bottom of the container before filling the soil.


Part 2

Planting Seeds


   Image titled Grow Flowers from Seed Step 5

   1

   Seed your seeds 4-8 weeks before you plan to move them to the garden. Usually, this means starting to grow 4-8 weeks before the final freezing estimate. Thus, the seeds have enough time to develop before being planted so that they are strong enough against the wind, insects, and other pests.

   Image titled Grow Flowers from Seed Step 6

   2

   Choose your land. Peat moss pellets work well with sprout trays, but good soil may be needed if you use a large container. Buy packaged soil at a gardening shop to ensure high-quality "sterile" mixtures. Many shops even sell mixed land for "new seeds".

   Image titled Grow Flowers from Seed Step 7

   3

   Fill the container up to ¾ full of soil. Do not solidify the soil in a container. Just leave it a little free.

   Image titled Grow Flowers from Seed Step 8

   4

   Moisten the soil before sowing the seeds. Give enough water to get wet, sponge-like consistency, but don't soak your soil.

   Image titled Grow Flowers from Seed Step 9

   5

   Follow the instructions on the seeds when sowing. Some flowers require that you cover the seeds with 2.5 cm of soil, while others may only need to be sown on the ground. Do not solidify the soil on top of the seedlings because it will strangle the seedlings and make the shoots difficult to penetrate the soil.

   Image titled Grow Flowers from Seed Step 10

   6

   Seed 2-3 seeds into each container. Give a distance of 0.5 cm to prevent seeds from disturbing each other.

   Image titled Grow Flowers from Seed Step 11

   7

   Give water again. Just pour a little water to moisturize the seeds and your new soil. Don't sink the seeds.

   Image titled Grow Flowers from Seed Step 12

   8

   Cover your container. The sprout tray should also be equipped with a clear plastic cover. If not, use clear plastic wrap. This protector prevents the seeds from drying out, but must be clear so that they can be penetrated by sunlight.


Part 3

Take care of your seedlings


   Image titled Grow Flowers from Seed Step 13

   1

   Place the seeds that are sown in locations with good air flow. The location must be well ventilated. However, if you want to prevent the air from getting too stagnant, turn on the electric fan in the seedling room, but don't direct it to your seeds. The fan can only be turned on for a few minutes.

   Image titled Grow Flowers from Seed Step 14

   2

   Follow the instructions on the packaging regarding temperature and sunlight. Usually, the seeds are placed near the window but not exposed to direct sunlight.

   Image titled Grow Flowers from Seed Step 15

   3

   Add additional sunlight if needed. Some seeds need 16 hours of sunlight a day to develop well. If so, turn on the incandescent lamp a few centimeters above the highest leaf when there is no sunlight.

   Image titled Grow Flowers from Seed Step 16

   4

   Lift the lid of the container after the seedlings show two complete sets of leaves. Move the sprouts to locations that get better sunlight.

   Image titled Grow Flowers from Seed Step 17

   5

   Rotate containers every 2-3 days. This prevents the seedlings from bending in one direction to reach sunlight.

   Image titled Grow Flowers from Seed Step 18

   6

   Give water with soft water. Use a watering bottle if possible. Soft watering prevents damage to the fragile and developing root system.

   Image titled Grow Flowers from Seed Step 19

   7
