9. I don't know how to create an email account. Can you tell me how to do it?


Ungkapan giving instruction biasanya pakai imperative sentence atau kalimat perintah yang bernada positif. Contoh:

Peel the carrots and cut into cube.

(Kupas wortel-wortelnya dan potong kotak-kotak.)

Wash the spinach then boil for 2 minutes.

(Cuci bayam lalu rebus selama 2 menit.)

Dalam percakapan, kita bisa menambahkan kata 'please' supaya bisa sopan.

Supaya berurutan, kita bisa pakai kata-kata berikut:

First, ...Second, ...Third, ...Then...Next...After that...Finally, ...

Contoh lengkap giving instruction:

How to cook instant noodles

1. First, boil 2 glasses of water in a pot.

2. Second, add noodles, breaking up if desired.

3. Then, cook for 3 minutes or until noodles are tender, stirring occasionally.

4. After that, remove from heat and stir in seasoning from flavor.

5. Finally, enjoy your noodle.

13. how to open a bank account teks procedure ​


Jawaban:

Opening a bank account typically involves a series of steps and requirements. Here's a general procedure to open a bank account:

1. Research and choose a bank: Start by researching different banks in your area or online. Consider factors like the bank's reputation, services offered, fees, and convenience.

2. Gather necessary documents: Banks require certain documents to open an account. Generally, you'll need the following:

- Proof of identity: Valid government-issued identification such as a passport, driver's license, or national ID card.

- Proof of address: Documents like utility bills, rental agreements, or bank statements that confirm your residential address.

- Social Security Number (SSN) or Tax Identification Number (TIN).

- Employment details: Information about your employer, job title, and income source.

3. Visit the bank: Once you've chosen a bank, visit the nearest branch in person. Schedule an appointment if required, especially during busy times.

4. Speak to a bank representative: Inform the bank representative that you want to open a bank account. They will guide you through the process and provide you with the necessary forms to fill out.

5. Fill out the application form: Complete the application form provided by the bank. The form will require personal details such as your name, address, date of birth, occupation, and contact information.

6. Submit the required documents: Present your identification documents, proof of address, and any other required documents to the bank representative. They will verify the information and make copies for their records.

7. Choose an account type: Banks offer various types of accounts, such as savings accounts, current accounts, or specialized accounts. Discuss your requirements and select the type of account that best suits your needs.

8. Deposit initial funds: Some banks require an initial deposit to activate the account. Determine the minimum deposit amount and provide the funds to the bank representative. They will credit it to your new account.

9. Review account terms and sign agreements: Carefully read any account agreements, terms, and conditions provided by the bank. Seek clarification if you have any questions. Once satisfied, sign the necessary documents to finalize the account opening process.

10. Receive account details: After completing the paperwork, the bank will provide you with your account details, including the account number, debit card, and any other relevant information.

11. Set up online banking (optional): If the bank offers online banking services, inquire about the procedure to set up online access. This will allow you to manage your account online and perform various banking transactions.

Jawaban:

1. A new dwarf planet.

Pluto, Pluto was declassified to a 'dwarf planet' in 2005, when a bigger planet was discovered.

Until 2005, the finest minds in space exploration had decided there were nine planets in our solar system. As far as everyone was concerned Pluto was the ninth but that all changed with the discovery of Eris, a planet which was 27 per cent bigger than Pluto.

Both Pluto and Eris were then declassified as 'dwarf planets', and the number of official planets in our solar system dropped from nine to eight.

2. The discovery of exoplanets.

The exoplanet Kepler-452b was discovered earlier this year and has very close similarities to earth.

Exoplanets are planets that obit a different star other than our Sun - in a different solar system to the one we are in. Even though scientists thought for a long time that they must exist, it was only in 1992 that the first exoplanet was discovered. And further work to look for other ones has turned up some exciting results more recently too. Earlier in 2015, scientists discovered the exoplanet Kepler-452b, which was described as 'earth's cousin' because of its close similarities to our planet.

Space experts say Exoplanets are really important because they raise the possibility that other life could exist in other solar solar systems.

3. Proving black holes exist.

The idea of of a black hole has been around for centuries, but for years it was difficult for scientists to prove they existed. Then, in 1990, the super strong Hubble telescope was launched into space. It was designed to take clear pictures of the deepest parts of space and these pictures have helped to prove the existence of black holes. Hubble can't see black holes themselves, but it can see where a black hole has pulled in matter from around it.

Black holes are believed to be dying stars that collapse inward from their own weight. Their pull of gravity becomes so strong that even light can't escape, which is why it looks black.

4. Exploring Mars for the first time.

In 1976, Nasa's Viking mission became the first successful spacecraft to land on Mars. It returned amazing colour pictures, soil samples and extensive scientific information about the planet. The craft also carried out an experiment which, for the first time, suggested that life might be possible on Mars. Exploration on Mars has continued with the Curiosity Rover and more recently the announcement that liquid water was found on the red planet's surface. Scientists say this raises the possibility that life could exist on the planet.

5. The first landing on a comet.

Rosetta mission to a comet, The Rosetta mission became the first to successfully land on a comet in 2014. It was seen as an historic moment as it allowed scientists to study the materials that make up a comet for the first time. Ongoing tests from the comet hope to find out more about how the universe was formed.

Radio waves from an alien world

The planets in the Solar System actually emit radio waves, even Jupiter as the largest planet in the Solar System has a very strong magnetic field compared to other planets around it, including Earth.

For the first time scientists detected radio emission from a planet from outside the solar system or in the world of astronomy, it is called an "extrasolar" (not orbiting the Sun).

Researchers suspect that the radio emissions came from Tau Boötes b, an exoplanet that is 51 light years from Earth.

X-rays exploded from the Milky Way galaxy

Many years ago, an explosion in the center of the Milky Way galaxy ejected energetic matter above and below the galactic "disk". The material still appears to glow on the gamma-ray spectrum in two blobs discovered in 2010, which are also known as Fermi bubbles.

In 2020, researchers found another pair of clumps in the same area visible on the X-ray spectrum. Researchers named this new discovery the eROSITA bubble.

Earth arrival "Mini-moon"

Earth arrives "Mini-moon" in October 2020. Not many people know about this phenomenon. Mini-moon is a rare object, other than an asteroid, that circled the Earth for some time before finally leaving Earth.

Astronomers named their discovery object as 2020 SO. This object is the booster for the Centaur rocket that was left in space by Surveyor 2 (NASA's robot that explored the Moon). And this is the second Mini-moon discovered in 2020, after 2020 CD3 left Earth in early 2020.

The discovery of the "ghost circle"

Scientists often find objects such as fuzzy blobs. But the ORC (Odd Radio Circle) invented in 2020 is something special. The blob is not a supernova remnant or an optical effect known as Einstein's rings.

Scientists think they are part of a "worm hole" (worm-holes), but no one knows for sure what they have found.

There is life on Venus

Venus, in terms of size and gravity, is almost similar to Earth. But Venus is not a very friendly place to live, because its atmosphere is made up of 96 percent CO2 and extreme temperatures.

In 2020, scientists discovered phosphine gas. On Earth, phosphine gas is produced by bacteria that live in oxygen-poor environments.

And for astronomers, phosphine gas is a preliminary finding on suspicion of life elsewhere in space.

Therefore the discovery of phosphine gas in the atmosphere of Venus is an early indication of life on the planet.
