1. Procedures use action verbs. Match the verbs in A with the meaning in B


Language comprehension requires rapid and flexible access to information stored in long-term memory, likely influenced by activation of rich world knowledge and by brain systems that support the processing of sensorimotor content. We hypothesized that while literal language about biological motion might rely on neurocognitive representations of biological motion specific to the details of the actions described, metaphors rely on more generic representations of motion. In a priming and self-paced reading paradigm, participants saw video clips or images of (a) an intact point-light walker or (b) a scrambled control and read sentences containing literal or metaphoric uses of biological motion verbs either closely or distantly related to the depicted action (walking). We predicted that reading times for literal and metaphorical sentences would show differential sensitivity to the match between the verb and the visual prime. In Experiment 1, we observed interactions between the prime type (walker or scrambled video) and the verb type (close or distant match) for both literal and metaphorical sentences, but with strikingly different patterns. We found no difference in the verb region of literal sentences for Close-Match verbs after walker or scrambled motion primes, but Distant-Match verbs were read more quickly following walker primes. For metaphorical sentences, the results were roughly reversed, with Distant-Match verbs being read more slowly following a walker compared to scrambled motion. In Experiment 2, we observed a similar pattern following still image primes, though critical interactions emerged later in the sentence. We interpret these findings as evidence for shared recruitment of cognitive and neural mechanisms for processing visual and verbal biological motion information. Metaphoric language using biological motion verbs may recruit neurocognitive mechanisms similar to those used in processing literal language but be represented in a less-specific way.

Keywords: sentence processing, verbal semantics, point-light walkers, biological motion, metaphor

A central question in the cognitive neuroscience of language is how meaning is represented and accessed in the brain during language comprehension and production. It has been well established that language and perception interact in the brain and in behavior. For instance, even after the presentation of a single word, semantically related words are processed more quickly (i.e., primed), possibly due to spreading activation of features shared between the two words (Anderson, 1983), and semantic priming occurs across mixed input and target modalities, such that pictures can also prime related words, and vice versa (Sperber et al., 1979). In the domain of action perception, a great deal of research supports the notion that visually perceiving actions and processing language about actions rely on overlapping representations in the mind and brain (Barsalou, 1999; Glenberg and Kaschak, 2002; Bergen et al., 2007). Here, we set out to explore the relationship between processing language about biological motion and visually perceiving it. To this end, we asked how a visual prime depicting biological motion would affect reading times of literal and metaphorical language containing action verbs with either a close or more distant semantic relationship to the prime.

Literal meaning is dependent not only on information stored in semantic memory but also on the physical context, including representations that are grounded in perception and action (Barsalou, 1999). 8. Change the verbs in the bracket into the present verbs form!


Ubahalah kata kerja di dalam kurung ke dalam bentuk present!!!

SIMPLE PRESENT
=> Simple present adalah tenses yang digunakan untuk menjelaskan kegiatan/perbuatan yang terjadi di masa sekarang. Dimana rumus nya :
(+) S + V1 (s/es) + O
(-) S + Do/does + not + V1 + O
(?) Do/does + S + V1 + O

Catatan!!!
* Tambahan s/es pada kalimat (+) dan penggunaan DOES pada kalimat (-) atau (?) hanya apabila subject nya orang ketiga tunggal seperti she/he/i/etc.

* Penggunaan verb-1 tanpa tambahan pada kalimat (+) dan penggunaan DO pada kalimat (-) atau (?) hanya apabila subject nya I/you/they/we/plural subject.


So, jawabannya :
1). The bell (RINGS).
2). The boys (TALK) in the classroom.
3). The policemen always (CATCH) the thieves.
4). The girl (SINGS) a song in the bathroom.
5). My uncle always (TURNS) on the air conditioner in the afternoon.
6). Grandfather (CULTIVATES) his rice field every day.
7). The leaves (FALL) on the ground.
8). The sun (SHINES) so brightly today.
9). The dogs (BARKS) loudly.
10). The women here (BUY) vegetables in the supermarket.

Catatan :
=> Dari subject di atas yang merupakan plural subject adalah :
* The boys (karena ada tambahan -s)
* The policemen (bentuk plural irregular dari "the policeman")
* The leaves (bentuk plural dari "leaf")
* The dogs
* The women (bentuk plural irregular dari "The woman")
=> Maka kata kerja nya tidak berubah atau tidak ada tambahan s/es.

Discussion:

Kata kerja adalah kata yang menerangkan suatu kegiatan yang dilakukan. Kata kerja disebut juga verba (verb).

Dalam kata kerja tergolong ke dalam dua bentuk:

kata kerja aksi (action verb) yaitu suatu pekerjaan yang dilakukan dengan wujud nyatakata kerja mental (mental verb) yaitu sesuatu yang dilakukan dengan pemikirannya/perasannya dengan wujud abstrak

Kata kerja juga memiliki dua bentuk berdasarkan kata dasarnya:

kata kerja biasa (regular verb) yaitu kata kerja yang tidak terdapat perubahan kata dasarnya (cth: look, know)kata kerja tidak biasa (irregular verb) yaitu kata dasarnya terdapat perubahan dari memerhatikan bentuk kalimatnya (cth: get, do)

Kata kerja memiliki lima bentuk berdasarkan kontekstual kalimatnya:

infinitive verb merupakan kata kerja dari suatu pekerjaan dengan tanpa perubahanpast simple verb merupakan kata kerja dari suatu pekerjaan dilakukan pada masa lalupast participle merupakan kata kerja dari suatu pekerjaan yang benar-benar sudah dilakukan dan ditempatkan pada bentuk pasifgerund atau disebut juga infinitive-ing merupakan kata kerja yang sedang dipraktikkan

Explanation:

Menemukan kata kerja pada dialog yang diberikan.

Eli: You look (verb) very happy. What's up?

—> Eli menanyakan ada apa dengan fisik Sam yang terlihat perasaannya sangat bahagia. Ini merupakan:

mental verbregular verbinfinitive verb

Sam: I got (verb) 80 for my English test.

—> dirinya yang mendapatkan nilai 80. Ini merupakan:

action verbirregular verbpast simple verb

Dan berikutnya ditemukan:

donethanksknowget

16. Past Tense, Change the verbs in​


Jawaban:

1. Bob and I (be) at school yesterday.

(+) Bob and I were at school yesterday.

(-) Bob and I weren't at school yesterday.

(?) Were Bob and I at schoo yesterday?

2. I (have) breakfast this morning.

(+) I had breakfast this morning.

(-) I hadn't breakfast this morning.

(?) Had I breakfast this morning?

3. We (be) on time this morning.

(+) We were on time this morning.

(-) We weren't on time this morning.

(?) Were we on time this morning?

Penjelasan:

Simple past tense mengubah bentuk verb menjadi bentuk kedua. Namun apabila bentuk kalimatnya negatif (-) atau bentuk kalimatnya interogatif (?), maka bentuk verbnya tetap menjadi bentuk pertama.

Bentuk verb sendiri ada dua jenis, yakni:

Reguler verb: Dance - danced - dancedIrreguler verb: Speak - spoke - spoken

Sedangkan bentuk to be jika dalam keadaan lampau, maka menggunakan was dan were.

Examples:

Diana was at the mosque yesterday.

We were on time this morning.
