35. How do you describe R.A kartini


Raden Adjeng Kartini is someone from the priyayi or Javanese aristocracy, the daughter of Raden Mas Adipati Ario Sosroningrat, the bupati of Jepara. She is the daughter of the first wife, but not the first wife. His mother was named M.A. Ngasirah, the daughter of Nyai Haji Siti Aminah and Kyai Haji Madirono, a religious teacher in Telukawur, Jepara. From his father's side, the Kartini lineage can be traced to Hamengkubuwana VI. Kartini's father was originally a wedana in Mayong. The colonial rule of that time required that a bupati be a noble. Because M.A. Ngasirah is not noble [2], then his father remarried with Raden Adjeng Woerjan (Moerjam), the direct descendant of King Madura. After the marriage, Kartini's father was appointed regent in Jepara to replace the position of R.A. Woerjan, R.A.A. Tjitrowikromo. Kartini is the fifth child of 11 siblings and stepbrothers. Of all siblings, Kartini is the eldest daughter. His grandfather, Prince Ario Tjondronegoro IV, was appointed bupati at the age of 25 years. Kartini's sister, Sosrokartono, is a smart linguist. Until the age of 12, Kartini is allowed to attend the ELS (Europese Lagere School). Here, among others, Kartini learned Dutch. But after the age of 12, he must stay home because it can dipingit.
Since Kartini can speak Dutch, then at home she starts to study herself and write letters to friends of correspondence originating from the Netherlands. One of them is Rosa Abendanon who supports it a lot. From European books, newspapers and magazines, Kartini is interested in the progress of European women's thinking. His desire was to promote indigenous women, for he saw that native women were in a low social status. Kartini reads a lot of Semarang De Locomotief's newspaper, which is nurtured by Pieter Brooshooft, she also receives leestrommel (a magazine package distributed by the bookstore to subscriptions). Among them are quite heavy cultural and scientific magazines, also Dutch women's magazine De Hollandsche Lelie. Kartini then later submitted several posts and published in De Hollandsche Lelie. From his letters it appears that Kartini reads everything intently, while making notes. Sometimes Kartini mentions one essay or quotes a few sentences. His attention is not only about the emancipation of women, but also of general social problems. Kartini sees the women's struggle for freedom, autonomy and equality of law as part of a wider movement. Among the books Kartini read before the age of 20, there was the title of Max Havelaar and the Love Letters of Multatuli, which he had read twice in November 1901. Then De Stille Kraacht (The Power of the Unseen) by Louis Coperus. Then Van Eeden's high-quality work, the mediocre Augusta de Witt, the feminist works of Mrs Goekoop de-Jong Van Beek and a Beram Von Suttner anti-war romance, Die Waffen Nieder (Put Weapons). All speak Dutch.
By his parents, Kartini was told to marry the Rembang regent, K.R.M. Duke Ario Singgih Djojo Adhiningrat, who has had three wives. Kartini married on November 12, 1903. Her husband understood Kartini's wishes and Kartini was given freedom and supported establishing a girls school east of the gate of the Rembang district office complex, or in a building now used as a Scout Building. His first and last child, Soesalit Djojoadhiningrat, was born on September 13, 1904. A few days later, September 17, 1904, Kartini died at the age of 25 years. Kartini is buried in Bulu Village, Bulu District, Rembang.
Thanks to his persistence Kartini, then founded the School of Women by Kartini Foundation in Semarang in 1912, and then in Surabaya, Yogyakarta, Malang, Madiun, Cirebon and other areas. The name of the school is "Kartini School". Kartini Foundation was founded by the family of Van Deventer, a figure of Ethical Policy.
In the letters Kartini written his thoughts about the social conditions at that time, especially about the condition of indigenous women. Most of his letters contain complaints and lawsuits particularly concerning the culture in Java which is seen as an obstacle to women's progress. He wants woman to have the freedom to gain knowledge and study.
