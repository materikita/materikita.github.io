1. the picture of the “Garuda” was found in various temples built between sixth and sixteenth centuries
2.  'Unity in Diversity' or, 'We are of many kinds, but we are one'. This motto is a founding principle of the modern Indonesian nation, which declares the essential unity of its members despite ethnic, regional, social or religious differences.
3. the “Garuda” symbolize creative energy
4. the feathers denote the date, the month and the year of Indonesia’s independence, the seventeenth of August, nineteen forty five.
5. the motto “Bhinneka Tunggal Ika” on banner held by the talons of the eagle means “Unity in Diversity” the motto symbolizes the unity of the Indonesian people in spite of their diverse cultural backgrounds.
6.the concept of BHINNEKA TUNGGAL IKA is not new to Indonesian history. It can be traced back to the time of the construction of Borobudur, when the Sailendra dynasty ruled on the plains of central java in the eighth and ninth centuries. 2 hundred years later, in the Brantas Valley in East Java, King Airlangga built a united kingdom based on this same principle. It was, however, the 14th century poet sage of Majapahit, Empu Tantular, who is said to have committed the phrase to writing for the first time.
7. the bar across the centre of the shield represents the equator that passes through Indonesia’s islands of Sumatra, Kalimantan, Sulawesi, and Halmahera.
8.  the chain is the symbol of the second principle: just and civilized humanity. 
9. paddy and cotton symbolizes the fifth principle, social justice for all the people of Indonesia
10. pancasila:
1. Believe in the one supreme GOD
2. Justice and civilized humanity
3. The unity of Indonesia
4. The democracy led by understanding wisdom among honorable representatives from the parliament house
5. Social justice for all of the people of Indonesia
