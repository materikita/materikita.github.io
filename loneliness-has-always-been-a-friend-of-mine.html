4. I went to see a friend of mine. jika kata ini di ubah menjadi negatif maka bagaimana perubahannya


--•Simple Past Tense•--
Responsio :
Pada dasarnya, cara mengubah kalimat positive dalam simple past tense hanya dengan menambah kata did not atau didn't setelah subject (apapun subjectnya) dan mengubah verb nya dari verb 2 menjadi verb 1 atau verb base.

Dalam kalimat ini verb went menjadi didn't go

(+) I went to see a friend of mine
(-) I didn't go to see a friend of mine

Reading Comprehension

In every linguistic exercise, there must be a text that evaluates our language understanding, it improves our treasury of language vocabularies, that's why reading or conversation is necessary in every language learning. And now we are going to read the text and answer the questions provided below.

MEETING MY IDOL

Afgan has always been my favourite singer. I had always been thinking of how I would feel when I met him. Then I was suddenly hit by lightning when I found out Afgan was coming to town for a concert in a local auditorium. A day before the concert, there would be a meet - and - greet event at a local radio station.

Feeling excited, I packed all my Afgan’s CDs to get his signature at the event. On that bright and sunny Saturday morning, the radio station was full of Afganism (that’s how Afgan’s fans are called). They sat on the chairs prepared inside the radio station’s lobby. Some stood in rows in the front yard of the radio station. A spot inside a lobby was prepared with a mini stage for Afgan’s singing performance and a table for Afgan to sign Afganism’s memorabilia.

Finally, after about 40 or 50 minutes wait, Afgan showed up from inside the radio station. He smiled and waved to all Afganism who had been waiting excitedly saying, “Good morning. How are you all?”. The crowd went crazy. The shouts sounded like a mix of “Fine, thank you” and screams of Afgan’s name.

Then, he started the event by singing his hit single “Dia dia dia”. Afganisme went even crazier; they sang along with him throughout the song. Of course, I did too. I couldn’t take my eyes off this amazing singer who had released three albums.

When he was finished with the song, the host announced that it was time for autographing the memorabilia. I prepared my CDs and began to stand in the line. When I arrived at the table, I was speechless. It was unreal just seeing him that close. I thought it was really cool seeing him like that because he really just felt like a normal person, which was awesome. He asked my name so that he could write it on the CD to say “To Mia, Love Afgan”. He was also very friendly, so I didn’t feel too nervous when I had a chance to take pictures with him. He was just an amazing person. And it was the best day ever!

Matter (Pembahasan)

COMPREHENSION QUESTIONS

1. How did the writer feel when she knew that Afgan was coming to town?

The writter felt so excited

2. Did the writer want to see the concert?

Yes, She did. She wanted to see Afgan's concert

3. When and where was the meet - and - greet event?

A day before the concert, there would be a meet - and - greet event at a local radio station.

4. What is Afganism?

Afghanism is the name of Afghan's fans club

5. How did the fans wait for Afgan?

They sat on the chairs prepared inside the radio station’s lobby. Some stood in rows in the front yard of the radio station. A spot inside a lobby was prepared with a mini stage for Afgan’s singing performance and a table for Afgan to sign Afganism’s memorabilia.

6. What did Afgan do when he showed up in the lobby?

Afgan showed up from inside the radio station. He smiled and waved to all Afganism who had been waiting excitedly saying, “Good morning. How are you all?”

The crowd went crazy. The shouts sounded like a mix of “Fine, thank you” and screams of Afgan’s name. Then, he started the event by singing his hit single “Dia dia dia”. Afganisme went even crazier; they sang along with him throughout the song

8. How did the writer feel when she finally got the turn to get Afgan’s

signature?

She was speechless to see Afghan and she thought Afghan was really cool

No, she did not
