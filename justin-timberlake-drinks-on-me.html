To find the fraction of her allowance that Jenny spends on food and drinks altogether, we need to add the fractions representing the amounts she spends on food and drinks.

Given:

Jenny spends 1/3 of her weekly allowance on food.

Jenny spends another 1/6 of her weekly allowance on drinks.

1/3 + 1/6

To add these fractions, we need to have a common denominator. The least common multiple (LCM) of 3 and 6 is 6. So, let's rewrite the fractions with the common denominator:

1/3 = 2/6 (multiplied by 2/2)

1/6 = 1/6

2/6 + 1/6 = 3/6

The fraction of her allowance that Jenny spends on food and drinks altogether is 3/6. However, we can simplify this fraction:

3/6 = 1/2
